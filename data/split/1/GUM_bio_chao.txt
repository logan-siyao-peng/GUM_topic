1	Early life
2	Born in Tianjin with ancestry in Changzhou , Jiangsu province ,
3	Chao went to the United States with a Boxer Indemnity Scholarship in 1910
4	to study mathematics and physics at Cornell University ,
5	where he was a classmate and lifelong friend of Hu Shih , the leader of the New Culture Movement .
6	He then became interested in philosophy ,
7	and earned a Ph.D. in philosophy from Harvard University in 1918 with a dissertation entitled " Continuity :
8	Study in Methodology " .
9	Already in college his interests had turned to music and languages .
10	He spoke German and French fluently and some Japanese ,
11	and he had a reading knowledge of ancient Greek and Latin .
12	He served as Bertrand Russell 's interpreter
13	when Russell visited China in 1920 .
14	In his My Linguistic Autobiography , he wrote of his ability
15	to pick up a Chinese dialect quickly , without much effort .
16	Chao possessed a natural gift
17	for hearing fine distinctions in pronunciation
18	that was said to be " legendary for its acuity " ,
19	enabling him to record the sounds of various dialects with a high degree of accuracy .

20	Career development and later life
21	He returned to China in 1920 ,
22	marrying the physician Yang Buwei there that year .
23	The ceremony was simple ,
24	rather than the noisy traditional wedding ,
25	attended only by Hu Shih and one other friend .
26	Hu 's account of it in the newspapers made the couple a model of modern marriage for China 's New Culture generation .
27	Chao taught mathematics at Tsinghua University and ,
28	one year later , returned to the United States
29	to teach at Harvard .
30	He again returned to China in 1925 ,
31	teaching at Tsinghua ,
32	and beginning a survey of the Wu dialects in 1926 .
33	He began to conduct linguistic fieldwork throughout China for the Institute of History and Philology of Academia Sinica from 1928 onwards .
34	During this period of time , he collaborated with Luo Changpei and Li Fang - Kuei , the other two leading Chinese linguists of his generation ,
35	to edit and render into Chinese Bernhard Karlgren 's monumental Etudes sur la Phonologie Chinoise
36	( published in 1940 ) .
37	He left for the US in 1938 ,
38	and resided there afterwards .
39	In 1945 , he served as president of the Linguistic Society of America ,
40	and a special issue of the society 's journal Language was dedicated to him in 1966 .
41	He became an American citizen in 1954 .
42	In the 1950s he was among the first members of the Society for General Systems Research .
43	From 1947 to 1960 , he taught at the University of California at Berkeley ,
44	where in 1952 , he became Agassiz Professor of Oriental Languages .
45	Both Chao and Yang were known for their good senses of humor ,
46	he particularly for his love of subtle jokes and language puns :
47	they published a family history
48	entitled , Life with Chaos :
49	the autobiography of a Chinese family .
50	Late in his life , he was invited by Deng Xiaoping to return to China in 1981 .
51	Previously at the invitation of Premier Zhou En - Lai , Chao and his wife returned to China in 1973 for the first time since the 1940s .
52	He visited China again between May and June in 1981
53	after his wife died in March the same year .
54	He died in Cambridge , Massachusetts .
55	His first daughter Rulan Chao Pian
56	( 1922 - 2013 )
57	was Professor of East Asian Studies and Music at Harvard .
58	His third daughter Lensey ,
59	born in 1929 ,
60	is a children 's book author and mathematician .
