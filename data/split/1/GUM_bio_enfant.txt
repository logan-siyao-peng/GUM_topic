13	Early life and education
14	L'Enfant was born in Paris , France on August 2 , 1754 , the third child and second son of Pierre L'Enfant
15	( 1704 – 1787 ) ,
16	a painter with a good reputation in the service of King Louis XV of France ,
17	( 1710 - 1774 ,
18	reigned 1715 - 1774 ) ,
19	and Marie L'Enfant , the daughter of a minor official at court .
20	In 1758 , his brother Pierre Joseph died at the age of six ,
21	and Pierre Charles became the eldest son .
22	He studied art at the Royal Academy in the Louvre , as well as with his father at the Royal Academy of Painting and Sculpture .
23	He left school in France
24	to enlist in the American Revolutionary War on the side of the rebelling colonials .
25	Military service
26	L'Enfant was recruited by Pierre Augustin Caron de Beaumarchais
27	to serve in the American Revolutionary War in the United States .
28	He arrived in 1777 at the age of 23 ,
29	and served as a military engineer in the Continental Army with Major General Lafayette .
30	He was commissioned as a captain in the Corps of Engineers on April 3 , 1779
31	to rank from February 18 , 1778 .
32	Despite his aristocratic origins ,
33	L'Enfant closely identified with the United States ,
34	changing his first name from Pierre to Peter
35	when he first came to the rebelling colonies in 1777 .
36	L'Enfant served on General George Washington 's staff at Valley Forge .
37	While there ,
38	the Marquis de Lafayette commissioned L'Enfant to paint a portrait of Washington .
39	He was wounded at the Siege of Savannah on October 9 , 1779 .
40	He recovered
41	and became a prisoner of war at surrender of Charleston , South Carolina on May 12 , 1780 .
42	He was exchanged in November 1780
43	and served on General George Washington 's staff for the remainder of the American Revolution .
44	L'Enfant was promoted by brevet to Major in the Corps of Engineers on May 2 , 1783 , in recognition of his service to the cause of American liberty .
45	He was discharged
46	when the Continental Army was disbanded in December 1783 .
47	After the war , L'Enfant designed the badge of the Society of the Cincinnati , an organization of former officers of the Continental Army ,
48	shaped as an eagle ,
49	at the request of Washington .
50	He was sent to France
51	to have insignias made for members of the Society , a group of veterans of the war .
52	Career
53	Post–Revolutionary War
54	Following the American Revolutionary War ,
55	L'Enfant established a successful and highly profitable civil engineering firm in New York City .
56	He achieved some fame as an architect
57	by redesigning the City Hall in New York for the First Congress of the United States
58	( See :
59	Federal Hall ) .
60	He also designed furniture and houses for the wealthy as well as coins and medals ,
61	including the insignia of the Society of the Cincinnati .
62	He was also a friend of Alexander Hamilton ,
63	and some of their correspondances from 1790 to 1801 now reside in the National Archives .
64	While L'Enfant was in New York City ,
65	he was initiated into Freemasonry .
66	His initiation took place on April 17 , 1789 , at Holland Lodge No. 8 , F&AM ,
67	which the Grand Lodge of New York F&AM had chartered in 1787 .
68	L'Enfant took only the first of three degrees
69	offered by the Lodge
70	and did not progress further in Freemasonry .
